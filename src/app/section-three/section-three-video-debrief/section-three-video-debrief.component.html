How does intercultural sensitivity relate to empathy?

You just watched a video on cultural interactions and wrote out your thoughts about the activity. The final question is: how might this activity relate to empathy?

To answer this question, it may be helpful to reflect on a few additional questions related to this cultural simulation:

  • Looking back at your answers to the earlier questions, did you report the different cultures in positive, negative, or neutral terms?
  • Do you think it is difficult for people to describe behaviors of other groups in objective, nonjudgmental terms?
  • Do people ever question the cultural norms given to them?

These questions may conjure the idea of stepping outside of your mindset, and experience cultural differences from a different point of view. Once you do that, you may begin to see differences as more relative, rather than good or bad.

This is what connects empathy with intercultural sensitivity. In order to possess sensitivity toward other cultures, a person needs to first try to understand a different perspective and participate in the experience of another—the very definition of empathy.
