Introduction

The previous lessons defined empathy as a concept, discussed how empathy might look in your classrooms, and presented an activity that simulated a foreign language classroom from the perspective of the student. As a quick recap, do you recall the three categories of empathy?

  1. (Cognitive) : ability to know how another person thinks and feels, or their internal state. Can be achieved through a number of strategies such as perspective taking (placing yourself in the perspective of another person) and active listening.
  2. (Emotional) : also known as “affective,” the ability to feel how another person feels. Can also be achieved through perspective taking and active listening.
  3. (Compassionate) : takes a step beyond knowing and sharing, and moves a person to actually act upon their understanding of another’s thoughts and feelings.

Do you recall the definition of empathy?

Empathy can be described as the ability to participate in the experience of another, both cognitively and emotionally.

The previous lesson presented an activity placed you into the mindset of a student learning a new language. What was the name of this practice, used to further improve empathy? Perspective taking.

This lesson will relate more specifically to cultural differences, and how they might look in real life; the relationship between empathy and intercultural sensitivity; and how empathy plays a critical role in approaching those differences.
