Introduction

We now come to the final lesson in this series on empathy. The previous lessons defined empathy, took the perspective of a student in a classroom, and simulated a cultural interaction to learn more about how empathy relates to intercultural communication. The lessons demonstrated ways to develop empathy as a skill, and made the connection between empathy and intercultural sensitivity. Letâ€™s begin with a recap of these lessons.

Empathy can be defined in three categories: cognitive, emotional, and compassionate.

Empathy can be defined as the ability to participate in the experience of another, both cognitively and emotionally.

One way to improve empathy is through perspective taking, in which you place yourself in the mindset of another person.

Empathy is also related to intercultural sensitivity, which is the ability to distinguish cultural differences and experience them in cross-cultural communication.

This lesson will finalize our series on empathy with a model of intercultural sensitivity, which places empathy on a scale as part of a continual process.

Lesson 4: Empathy Through Ethnorelativism

The final module in this series on empathy will describe the concepts of ethnocentric and ethnorelative views, and how they connect back to empathy.
