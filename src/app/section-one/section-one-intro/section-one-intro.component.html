Introduction

We have all heard about empathy. We know it’s generally a good thing. We know it’s related to connecting with others, and knowing how to help them. More specifically as ESL instructors, we know that empathy is important to better understand our students, who likely have different cultural norms and backgrounds from our own.

But do we all define empathy in the same way? Does your concept of empathy differ from mine? Moreover, how can we understand and measure how empathic we really are?

This lesson will provide an overview of empathy as a concept, discussing the various ways it can be described, and honing in on a definition. The lesson will then turn to you, and provide an activity that will test your empathy and identify ways to increase it over time.

The lesson intends to answer the following questions:

What exactly is empathy?

How does empathy relate to my classroom?

Is empathy a trait or a skill?

How empathic am I, and how can I increase my empathy?

If you’re ready to start, let’s dive in! Proceed to the next section.

Lesson 1: Empathy Through Self-Awareness

This module explores empathy as a concept and uses it to describe an individual’s personal values and views, thus connecting the concept to self- awareness and assisting with empathizing with students in a classroom. (Estimated completion time: 1 hour)
